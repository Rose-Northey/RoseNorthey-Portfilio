Monster Mash

It takes two to make a truly terrifying beast

Live Link

Project Overview

This web-based app is an exquisite-corpse-type drawing game where a monster is drawn by two players and then saved to an interactive monster menagerie. This project was completed in less than one week and utilised a number of technologies not specifically learned in class.

My role in this project was scrum facilitator. My goal in this role was to create an empowering experience for all members of the group through clear communication. I focused on inclusivity and what resulted was a product that had clear contributions by all members.

Planning sessions included extensive use of a Kanban, visual mapping of the app structure, listening and looping ideas, and talking openly about feelings. This planning-heavy approach increased the quality of our time on keys, boosted the morale of the team with frequent kanban celebrations, and enabled each team member to feel as though they were able to contribute to the fullest.

Project Learnings

This project taught me the value of learning on demand. In the past, I have preferred to learn technology from the ground up to fully understand its complexities. Due to the short time frame, we instead opted for a quicker strategy. We quickly found code which did a similar thing to what we were looking for, gained a deep understanding of that code, and then integrated the found code into the rest of our system. I also gained an understanding of how important vulnerable conversations can be in the workplace - my team was able to tackle some fairly frustrating setbacks with optimism because we normalised feelings in group standups.

Project Improvements

This is an ongoing project! Thanks to the project being such a great experience, all team members are keen to keep iterating on the final result. So the following improvements are already in progress. We are very excited to make the HTML canvas responsive and to add authorisation and profiles to the experience so that monsters can be made collaboratively with users on different computers. This project could also have significant improvements to code quality as the short time frame lowered the priority of testing and thorough use of typescript - both of which will solve problems preemptively in the future.
